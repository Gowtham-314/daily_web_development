Indian Army

The Indian Army is the land-based branch and the largest component of the Indian Armed Forces, dedicated to safeguarding the nation’s sovereignty and integrity.

Founded in its modern form on January 26, 1950, it is renowned worldwide for its discipline, immense courage, and "Service Before Self" motto.

Soldiers of the Indian Army are known for their selfless service, often operating in challenging terrains—from the high altitudes of the Siachen Glacier to hot deserts—to protect the country's borders.

Beyond defending against external threats and maintaining internal security, the Army plays a crucial role in humanitarian relief during natural disasters and contributes significantly to United Nations peacekeeping missions globally.

It stands as a powerful symbol of unity, pride, and national security for all Indians
